Physical Characteristics & Adaptations

Speed: Cheetahs are the fastest land animals, capable of reaching speeds up to 70 mph (113 km/h) in short bursts covering distances up to 1,500 ft (460 m).
Build: They have slender bodies, long legs, a small rounded head on a long neck, and a deep chest. Their spine is highly flexible, aiding in their explosive speed.
Claws & Tail: Unlike most big cats, cheetahs have semi-retractable claws for better grip during high-speed chases. Their long tail helps with balance and steering.
Coloration: Their tan coat with black spots provides camouflage in grassy environments.


Behavior & Diet

Hunting Style: Cheetahs rely on sight rather than scent and prefer to hunt during the day (diurnal) to avoid competition from nocturnal predators like lions or hyenas.
Prey: They mainly hunt small to medium-sized ungulates such as gazelles, impalas, and young wildebeests.
Social Structure: Males may live in small groups called "coalitions," often made up of brothers. Females are generally solitary except when raising cubs.

Habitat, Range & Conservation

Habitat: Cheetahs prefer open savannas, grasslands, and sometimes dry forests. They need vast territories for hunting.
Geographic Range: Most cheetahs live in sub-Saharan Africa, with a small, critically endangered population in Iran.
Threats: Their population is threatened by habitat loss, human-wildlife conflict, poaching, and loss of genetic diversity.
Conservation Status: Cheetahs are listed as Vulnerable by the IUCN. There are fewer than 7,000 cheetahs left in the wild as of recent estimates.
Efforts: Conservation efforts include habitat preservation, anti-poaching measures, and breeding programs.
